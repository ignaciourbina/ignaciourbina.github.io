### Dissertation Research

**Three Essays on the Political Implications of AI**  
*Committee: Oleg Smirnov (Chair), Reuben Kline, Andrew Engelhardt*

1. **Ignacio Urbina**. The Conditional Activation Model: Uncovering Policy Demand Responses to AI-Driven Automation (Job Market Paper).
   - Introduces a "funnel of causality" framework explaining when citizens update policy preferences in response to AI-driven job automation risk. Uses pre-registered experiment with ~1,000 U.S. workers and Bayesian finite mixture model.

2. **Ignacio Urbina**. An Agent-Based Model of LLMs in Democratic Deliberation.
   - Explores AI-driven agents to enhance deliberative democracy in small community settings; uses agent-based modeling with LLM-powered virtual representatives.

3. **Ignacio Urbina** & Oleg Smirnov. The Nature of Attitudes Toward Artificial Intelligence in Mass Publics.
   - Examines the structure of public AI attitudes using exploratory factor analysis on nationally representative U.S. sample; reveals multidimensional structure across domains and contexts.
